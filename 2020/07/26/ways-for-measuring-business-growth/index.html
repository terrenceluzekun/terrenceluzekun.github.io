Three ways of measuring business growth

Business growth is defined as “The process of improving some measure of an enterprise success” (http://www.businessdictionary.com/definition/business-growth.html, 无日期) Before we measure the business growth we may firstly think about how to measure a business itself. In my opinion, number of people working in particular business is direct to indicate the size of a business because more labors could lead to larger production. Meanwhile, the market share of a business is also an indicator, larger percentage of market share means more power of controlling market.

In the beginning, I want to introduce some ways of measuring business growth before discussing the aims of measuring such growth. Firstly, the feedbacks from consumers could clearly reflect the change in some aspects in business such as quality of goods or service & prices of products from a business. Method for collecting consumer’s feedbacks includes questionnaire or other types of researches. If most of consumers think that the products are becoming better, it could be a kind of business growth. Secondly, the market share value may also be a good indicator while more shares in market or larger number of shareholders are the sign of expanding. More people purchasing shares in particular business means reputation of this business is better than before and the confidence of shareholders increase as well. I think these belong to business growth. Lastly, number of labors or capitals such as machines in an industry can indicate growth of business. Because labors and capitals are all factors of production, increase in these stuffs will lead to expansion of a business obviously, which is business growth.

After introducing three methods of measuring business growth, I think aims of measuring is conspicuous. Firms can adjust the ways of producing or managing through the measurement of growth, for example, if consumers are not satisfied with products of a firm after a period of time, change in quality of goods or prices of products are needed to be considered. Meanwhile, if little growth is achieved over a period, actions like hiring more labors or machines may need to take.

To sum up, I think frequent measurements of growth in business is necessary for formulate strategies. It is a very direct way to tell managers situation in a firm or industry and how to improve. Among the three methods above, I think collecting consumers’ feedbacks is the most useful because most of private businesses are profit maximized so production in business should be adjusted through consumers while they decide the amount of profit a business can make.
